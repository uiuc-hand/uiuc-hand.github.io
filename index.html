A Soft Tendon-Driven Dexterous Hand for Robust Teleoperation and Learning

Leo Lin1, Jay Moon1, Shivansh Patel1
1University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign
Video Code BOM

Abstract

We propose to develop a soft, tendon-driven dexterous hand that is inherently compliant. In contrast to existing dexterous hands, which are often fragile and prone to damage, our design leverages soft materials to improve durability and ease of use.

We will implement a control framework from the ground up and integrate it with a vision-based teleoperation system for intuitive manipulation. The hand will be mounted on an xArm, enabling coordinated control of both the arm and hand to replicate human-like actions.

We will evaluate the usability of our system against the open-source Leap V1 hand and demonstrate its potential in imitation learning and reinforcement learning applications.

Vision based teleoperation

To teleoperate the hand, we use HAMER, a vision-based transformer to extract MANO hand poses, and directly translate it to the hand.

Compliance

The soft TPU within the hand is able to deform and conform to the shape of objects it's colliding with, allowing for easier manipulation and is more resilient to damage.

TESTS

Repeatability testing

We can test how repeatable the hand is, showing very little error during continuous operation of the hand.
